The F-pattern is a common scanning pattern, particularly on text-heavy web pages like blogs or articles like this one. Users’ eyes generally move in the shape of the letter “F.” They start at the top left, move across the page to the right, down a bit, and then to the right again.

Top Bar: Place the most critical information, such as the logo and navigation menu, at the top. Users generally start scanning from here.

Headlines and Sub-headings: Use bold, clear headlines and sub-headings to break up long sections of text. This draws the reader’s attention as their eyes move down the page.

Left Alignment: Since eyes naturally go to the left side of the page, align important content to the left.

The Z-pattern is ideal for pages with less text and more visual content, such as landing pages or home pages. Eyes follow a “Z” shape: starting at the top left, moving to the right, then diagonally to the bottom left, and then to the right again.

Top Bar: Like the F-pattern, begin with crucial elements like the logo and navigation at the top.

Visuals: Use compelling images or graphics across the top and middle to guide the eyes from left to right.

Call to Action (CTA): Place a strong CTA at the final point of the Z, usually at the bottom right.
